Web Page Goals

This website has been created for the research purposes of a 4th year University dissertation that aims to understand how the public perceives biometric authentication. This website forms an aspect of the project where an educational tool is created and then tested to measure how successful it has been at improving the public’s understanding and knowledge of biometric authentication.

Educate

This website aims to act as an educational source on biometric authentication. By providing information on the topic, the goal is to successfully inform the public on the facts about biometric authentication.

Test

The website features a testing section that allows users to challenge themselves on how much they really know about biometric authentication.

Feedback

To understand how successful the website has been in achieving its goals, feedback is appreciated through the submission of test scores, along with any other comments that may be relevant.

How to use the website

This website features two main sections: the educate page and the test. To obtain accurate feedback on how successful this website is as an educational tool, please first take the test, which can be accessed by clicking ‘Test Yourself!’ on the navigation bar at the top of the screen. Once the test is complete, please take note of the score achieved and then return to the home page.

The next step is to go to the educate page (also accessible through the top navigation bar) and read through the information provided.

After reading through the information, re-take the ‘Test Yourself!’ test and again take note of the new score upon completion of the test. Finally, please submit both scores along with any feedback on how the educational tool could be improved through the survey link provided below.
